Interface Criticality
Interface criticality defines the operational impact of a failure or issue at a given interface. Each interface is assessed against the categories below to determine its criticality rating: HIGH, MEDIUM, LOW, or N/A.
Criticality Category Focus Definition Production Impact ICD Required?
HIGH Throughput & Safety An interface issue that shuts down a major choke point (e.g., Crusher, Train Loader, Automated Haulage System (AHS) block) for more than 4 hours, or compromises a Safety-Critical Function (e.g., collision avoidance, emergency stop system, blast control). This category includes any external interface (e.g., utility, regulatory) that prevents material movement or export. Direct, measurable loss of annualised production volume (Mt) or poses an immediate threat to personnel or major fixed assets. Yes
Decarbonisation Any issue that critically delays the Real Zero 2030 initiatives, such as the deployment of renewable energy infrastructure or the integration of new electric equipment. Long-term strategic risk to corporate commitment and license to operate.
MEDIUM Efficiency & Asset Health An interface issue that degrades processing or logistics efficiency (e.g., blending failure leading to off-spec ore, rail network congestion, Fleet Management System (FMS)/Automated Haulage System (AHS) partial system degradation) or requires an unplanned major maintenance intervention. This results in a sustained operational rate reduction of 10%–25% for a shift. Requires the activation of a pre-planned production contingency (e.g., using a stockpile bypass, reducing train speed, re-routing ore). Causes a moderate, trackable deviation from the monthly plan. Maybe
Cost Requires a significant, unbudgeted expenditure (e.g., 5% of the relevant project or operating budget) to resolve.  
LOW Minor Process & Support An interface issue that causes a minor, localised interruption that can be resolved by frontline teams within a single shift (e.g., I.T. network lag in a non-critical area, minor civil/drainage issue, small data incompatibility between two non-critical systems). Operational rate reduction is less than 10% and quickly recovered. Minor impact on daily targets but does not jeopardise the weekly or monthly plan. No
N/A N/A No measurable impact on safety, production, cost, or schedule, or the interface is fully defined, verified and validated. None. N/A
